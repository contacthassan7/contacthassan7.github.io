Core Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

First, I always consider life is a great big adventure filled with experiences. And it shows that failure is a part of our journey. Challenges and mistakes will help me learn. Throughout the foundations, I have growth mindset which enables me to stay positive and embrace new learnings. Secondly, higher EQ is essential and key to happiness for me. Modern day work is all about team collaboration. And this is where EQ plays very important role. I’m working on it and now very much awareness of my emotions. Third, I learned that for problem solving pseudo coding plays very important role. It expedites the problem solving process and ensure I understand the problem right.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

All these three elements play very important role. Value is who you are, what you stand for. Nobody is perfect so empathy gives better understanding of all colleagues I’ll be working with. While self-awareness, helps very much in understand yourself and recognizing the emotions going though. It gives a good check on mental health especially when doing the programming.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

For me learning on neuroplasticity was very surprising. I didn’t know that before. The principles of neuroplasticity were an opener for me. After that I think I can learn anything.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

For me as a new coder, decipher the query/problem was the most challenging.

Why do you think that we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

To my understanding, coding is essential, but doing it all alone will not take me anywhere. Core learning plays very important role and I feel lucky to have been introduced to such concepts. I think Dev Academy spending so much time focusing on core learning is for a reason which is to explore ourselves, aware of our situation, emotions and have a check on our mental health. It also help us understanding others and in practical life it’s very much important. IT environment operates in Agile fashion and a key to success in Agile environment is collaboration and understanding each other.

Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to start a business and get my own web development related projects. And for that I’ll gain experience first by getting into the market and sharpen my skills and build industry contacts. At the same time, I also would like to contribute to the community and I’m thinking either to do so by teaching voluntarily or pick up web development related project in Aotearoa.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strength would be my family, the way they all are supporting right now. I think mindfulness is also my biggest strength, this keeps anxiety at bay. I have discovered the rubber duck method works so well for me. My limitation is time and less clear guidelines. Mental exhaustion comes very easily in this part of life. I am slow to begin with, takes time to get understanding and experimentation.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Cover all the material in time and doing enough exercises to feel confident and comfortable would remain a challenge.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to develop communication and self-awareness skills. I would also like to work on collaboration part. I think collaboration is very essence to fast track the learning. I’m looking forward building that.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I would like to have support from the team where I’m stuck in a problem or when going tangent. On the other hand, checking on mental health is a must. I would expect the same as facilitators did in foundations.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will be taking regular breaks. Sometimes changing the environment works really well for me. I would also work on my communication with all in Bootcamp. This is the key.
