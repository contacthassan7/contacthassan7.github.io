What is emotional intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability to recognize, use, and control one's own emotions in a constructive way in order to reduce stress, communicate effectively, sympathize with others, overcome obstacles, and diffuse conflict. It helps in the development of stronger relationships, academic and professional success. It also helps in connecting with your emotions, putting intentions into action, and making informed decisions on what is most important to you.

Emotional intelligence is something that can be learnt and cultivated, not something that is fixed or innate, however some people may find it easier due to their upbringing or experience. And having a high level of Emotional Intelligence improves your ability to empathise and relate to others.

Four characteristics are used to characterize emotional intelligence are:

  • Self-management You can regulate your emotions in a healthy way, take initiative, follow through on commitments, and adjust to changing situations.
  • Self-awareness You're aware of your own feelings and how they influence your thoughts and actions. You are aware of your talents and flaws and are self-assured.
  • Social awarenessYou're empathetic. You are aware of the feelings, needs, and concerns of others.
  • Relationship management You understand how to build and maintain positive relationships, communicate effectively, inspire and influence others, collaborate effectively, and resolve disputes.

How is it different to IQ?

EQ is very different than IQ. Below are the few points to tell the difference:

  • EQ is the ability to recognize emotions while IQ is a person’s reasoning ability.
  • You can improve your EQ while IQ is something you are born with.
  • A person with high EQ can control and access emotions while a person with high IQ can swiftly understand the logical reasoning and solve complex problems.
  • A person’s success in school depends on his IQ level, whereas a person’s EQ level reflects his success in real life.
  • A person's IQ level determines his academic performance, whereas his EQ level reflects his real-life success.
